This page will become the final interpretive endpoint of the project after Stage 4 annotation is complete and the Stage 5-6 corpus-wide analysis has been run.

The expected final synthesis will answer four questions:

1. Which metaphor clusters most strongly organize Lincoln's Civil War rhetoric?
2. How does Lincoln's metaphor system make war feel obligatory rather than chosen?
3. Which agents are systematically absent from the roles the metaphors make available?
4. What structural difference separates Lincoln's violence-authorizing rhetoric from purification-based political pathology?

The working hypothesis is that Lincoln's rhetoric authorizes mass violence through obligation, sacrifice, proof, inheritance, and theodicy, but does not construct a social group as a contaminating pathogen requiring purification. That absence may be the central structural reason his rhetoric could move toward reconciliation.
