The Analysis section gathers the project's close readings and corpus-level interpretive structures. All pages reflect the complete 136-instance annotated corpus and the Stage 5-6 aggregate analysis.

Raw counts are descriptive, not sufficient publication evidence by themselves. Use [Controlled Outputs](analysis/controlled_outputs.md) to inspect every major aggregate pattern by register and by the `authorship_confidence >= 0.95` subset before treating it as stable.

Use these pages in this order:

1. [Systematic Absence](analysis/systematic_absence.md): tracks entities that Lincoln's metaphor system logically requires but does not fully grant agency.
2. [Diachronic Map](analysis/diachronic_map.md): maps how metaphor clusters shift across Lincoln's career.
3. [Controlled Outputs](analysis/controlled_outputs.md): tests aggregate cluster and absence claims by register, period, document, and authorship-confidence subset.
4. Cluster Profiles: summarize each of the six major metaphor clusters across the corpus.
5. Document Notes: preserve close readings of individual Lincoln texts.
6. [Lincoln vs. Hitler](comparison/koenigsberg_comparison.md): places the Lincoln findings in comparative relation to Koenigsberg's account of political pathology.
7. [LCC Benchmark Validation](analysis/lcc_validation.md): compares Lincoln's six clusters against the LCC Metaphor Dataset (8,724 general English annotations) to establish which of Lincoln's source-concept domains are common in English and which are Lincoln-specific constructions.

The analysis pages are meant to be read as a bridge between the sentence-level annotation data and the final synthesis. They show how local metaphors become larger structures of obligation, sacrifice, absence, and reconciliation.
