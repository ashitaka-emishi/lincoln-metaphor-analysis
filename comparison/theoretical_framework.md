# Theoretical Framework — CMT + Koenigsberg Integration

## Overview

This project applies two complementary theoretical frameworks to Lincoln's corpus:
1. **Conceptual Metaphor Theory** (Lakoff & Johnson, 1980) — the cognitive architecture of metaphor
2. **Koenigsberg's ideological fantasy analysis** — the psychological and political function of metaphor

Neither framework alone is sufficient. CMT maps the structure; Koenigsberg maps the function. Together they enable the project's central claim: that Lincoln's metaphor system made the Civil War feel *obligatory* rather than chosen, and that the specific structure of his metaphors explains both why the war was prosecutable and why reconciliation was conceivable.

---

## Part 1: Conceptual Metaphor Theory

### Core Claim
Metaphors are not rhetorical ornaments added to thought — they are the cognitive structures through which abstract political reality is understood. When Lincoln's audience hears "binding up the nation's wounds," they are not receiving a decorative image. They are receiving a cognitive schema that determines what the national situation *is* and what responses are possible.

### Key CMT Constructs

**Source domain / Target domain**: The concrete → abstract mapping. The source domain provides the cognitive structure; the target domain is structured by it.

**Entailments**: Inferences that transfer automatically from source to target. These are the ideological payload. The physician metaphor entails that someone must act, that delay is harmful, that the patient is passive, that repair is possible. None of these entailments are argued for — they are assumed by the mapping.

**Conventional vs. novel**: Conventional metaphors feel literal. "The body politic," "the founding fathers," "the nation's wounds" — these feel descriptive, not figurative. This is their power: the ideological work happens below the threshold of rhetorical awareness.

**Extended metaphors**: Some of Lincoln's most important deployments sustain a mapping across an entire speech (Gettysburg as extended birth narrative). The extension accumulates ideological force: each element of the mapping reinforces the others.

**Suppression**: When a metaphor that would normally be deployed in a given context is absent, the absence is structured. Track suppression events — what cluster should appear, and why it doesn't.

### What CMT Explains
- How Lincoln's metaphors work structurally (the mappings)
- What inferences the metaphors license (the entailments)
- Why the metaphors feel self-evident rather than constructed (conventionalization)
- How different clusters interact (co-activation, hierarchies)

### What CMT Doesn't Explain
- *Why* these particular mappings are politically powerful
- *What psychological work* they perform for audiences
- *Why* the violence they sanction feels necessary rather than chosen
- *Why* certain entities are systematically absent from the mappings

For these questions, Koenigsberg's framework is required.

## Part 2: Koenigsberg's Ideological Fantasy Analysis

### The Magical Object
Mass political violence is sustained not by rational calculation but by ideological fantasy — the unconscious treatment of a collective entity (the nation, the race, the party) as a "magical object": an extension of the collective self requiring violent defense. The magical object generates obligatory violence because threats to it feel like threats to the self.

In Lincoln's corpus, the magical objects are:
- The national body (cluster_01)
- The founding proposition (cluster_03)
- The constitutional covenant (cluster_02)
- The ancestral lineage (cluster_05)
- The divine instrument (cluster_06)

### The Obligatory Frame
The key mechanism: ideological fantasy makes violence feel mandatory rather than chosen. The wound must be healed. The oath must be enforced. The proposition must be proven. The ancestral debt must be paid. God's will must be accomplished.

The obligatory frame does not merely justify violence — it denies that it is a choice. Lincoln positions the war as something happening to him ("events have controlled me") as much as something he is doing.

### The Sacrificial Economy
In Lincoln's framework, soldier deaths are not waste — they yield: national identity, propositional proof, sin redemption, new birth. The sacrificial economy converts grief into investment. This is also manic defense (Kleinian): triumph over loss through the claim that death produces.

### Systematic Absence
The framework's ideology is revealed as much by what it cannot accommodate as by what it asserts. Enslaved people cannot be agents in Lincoln's metaphor system because the system was built on civic categories that excluded them. Black soldiers cannot be provers or inheritors because the system positions them as objects of the proposition, not its demonstrators.

### The Psychic Defense Layer
Optional Kleinian extension: idealization (founders), splitting (Union vs. Confederacy), projection (internal conflict externalized onto the South), manic defense (sacrificial economy), reparation (wound-healing as guilt-repair), displacement (slavery's cause displaced onto abstract principles).

## Part 3: CMT + Koenigsberg Integration

### How They Work Together

| CMT level | Koenigsberg level | Integration |
|-----------|------------------|-------------|
| Mapping structure | Fantasy type | The mapping determines which fantasy type is activated |
| Entailments | Violence logic | Entailments determine what the violence does (heals, proves, punishes) |
| Conventional/novel | Obligatory frame | Conventional metaphors make violence feel self-evident; novel ones call attention to the frame |
| Extended metaphor | Sacrificial economy | Extended metaphors sustain the sacrificial economy across a speech |
| Source domain | Projected entity | The source domain determines what is treated as the magical object |
| Suppression | Absence flags | Suppressed clusters and absent entities both reveal the system's limits |

### The Compound Analytical Claim

A fully analyzed metaphor instance specifies:
1. **What is mapped onto what** (CMT: cluster_id, source_domain, target_domain, entailments)
2. **What psychological structure the mapping activates** (Koenigsberg: fantasy_type, violence_logic, obligatory_frame, projected_entity)
3. **What moral economy it creates** (Koenigsberg: guilt_distribution, sacrificial_economy, sacrificial_yield)
4. **What it conceals** (Koenigsberg: absence_flags, absence_notes)
5. **What psychic work it performs** (Koenigsberg optional: psychic_defense)

## Part 4: Lincoln vs. Hitler — The Structural Question

### Shared structural elements
- Body-politic projection (national body as magical object)
- Obligatory frame (violence as necessary rather than chosen)
- Sacrificial economy (soldier deaths as productive)
- Ancestral debt (obligation to founding generation)

### Lincoln-specific elements
- Experiment and proof logic (violence as evidentiary — no Hitler parallel)
- Oath and obligation (civic covenant rather than racial bond)
- Distributed and cosmic guilt (guilt that enables reconciliation)
- Inscrutable Providence (morally humble theodicy)

### Hitler-specific elements
- Disease and purification logic (**absent from Lincoln** — the key structural finding)
- Ethnic body rather than civic body
- External guilt only (no distributed guilt, no reconciliation logic)
- Legible Providence (certain destiny rather than inscrutable judgment)

### The Purification-Logic Absence as Key Finding

**Hypothesis (confirm against corpus)**:

Lincoln's corpus contains zero instances of `disease_and_purification` fantasy type. No group is constructed as a pathogen that the national body must expel.

This absence is structural, not accidental. Slavery is constructed as a **wound** (reparable damage) and as a **sin** (requiring punishment and redemption), never as a **disease** (requiring the expulsion of a pathogen group). Enslaved people are constructed as passive (non-agents), never as threats to be eliminated.

**Why this matters**:
- Restorative violence (wound logic) has an exit: wounds heal
- Generative violence (birth logic) has an exit: children grow up
- Punitive violence (theodicy logic) has an exit: the punishment is paid
- Purifying violence has **no exit**: the pathogen is never fully expelled

This is the structural reason Lincoln's rhetoric could accommodate "malice toward none" and Hitler's could not. The architecture of Lincoln's metaphor system has off-ramps built in. Hitler's does not.

## Scope and Limitations

**Corpus scope**: 29 documents, 1838–1865. Wide by design to prevent cherry-picking.

**Method scope**: CMT + Koenigsberg analysis of metaphor instances. Does not include prosodic analysis, audience reception data, or comparative rhetorical analysis of contemporaries.

**The Hitler comparison is structural, not moral**: The project does not claim that Lincoln and Hitler are morally equivalent. It claims that their rhetoric shares certain structural features (obligatory frame, sacrificial economy, magical object) and differs in one decisive structural feature (purification logic). The shared features explain why mass political violence is possible under any political system that uses ideological fantasy; the difference explains why Lincoln's framework had an exit condition.

**Confidence limits**: All claims are provisional until confirmed against annotation data. Two pre-stated findings (enslaved people as non-agents; absence of disease_and_purification) must be confirmed or disconfirmed against actual corpus annotation.
