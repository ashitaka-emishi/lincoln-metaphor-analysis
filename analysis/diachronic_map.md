**Key observations from the table:**

1. **1858 is the corpus peak** (27 instances) driven almost entirely by cluster_05 (17) and cluster_01 (10). The Lincoln-Douglas debates are the most metaphorically dense documents in the corpus.

2. **1861 is the war-launch spike** (25 instances). Cluster_02 launches with 7 instances. Cluster_06 begins its growth with 6. Cluster_03 reactivates with 6 (dormant since 1852). Four clusters are active simultaneously for the first time.

3. **Cluster_05 disappears after 1863** (last attestation: Gettysburg, 1863-11-19). The founding-fathers frame is entirely absent in 1864 and 1865.

4. **Cluster_03 disappears after 1864** (last attestation: 1864-11-10). The experiment/proof frame never appears in the Second Inaugural or Last Address.

5. **Cluster_01 peaks in 1865** (8 instances — its largest single-year count, all from doc_021 Second Inaugural). The body/organism frame transforms in Phase 4 into its Reconstruction form: physician-president binding wounds.

6. **Cluster_06 is the only cluster that grows monotonically** through the war. It never declines. Its 5-instance 1865 count is the second-highest single-year figure for any cluster.

## Phase-by-Phase Narrative

### Phase 0: Baseline (1838–1853)

Three documents, all formal public addresses, total 16 instances. The metaphor system is in proto-form.

**Cluster_05 dominant**: 4 instances at the Lyceum (1838) establish the foundational inheritance argument — the founding generation bequeathed institutions to the present; the present owes them maintenance. This frame will become the largest cluster in the corpus (35 instances) before vanishing at Gettysburg.

**Cluster_03 earliest**: Also first attested at the Lyceum (1838), with 3 instances. The experiment/proof frame launches at the same moment as the founding-fathers frame — they are the Phase 0 co-anchors.

**Cluster_04 appears once**: The Temperance Address (1842) contains the only cluster_04 instances until Gettysburg (1863). Liberty-as-germ, cradle-of-liberty — the birth frame appears in a domestic moral-reform context 21 years before its canonical political deployment.

**Cluster_06 first attested**: Clay Eulogy (1852) provides the first Providence instances — providential provision of great men for national crises. The theodicy frame begins in a eulogistic register as a consolation argument.

---

### Phase 1: Argument (1854–1860)

The anti-extension argument. Eight documents, heavy debate register. 42 instances — the second-largest phase by count.

**Cluster_05 at peak**: 17 of 35 total cluster_05 instances are in the debate year alone (1858). The founding-fathers inheritance frame is the primary anti-extension resource: Lincoln does not argue that slavery is evil (moral radicalism) but that extending it betrays the founders (conservative constitutionalism). Cooper Union (1860) deploys the founders as direct legal witnesses with maximum historical specificity.

**Cluster_01 intensifies**: 10 instances in 1858 — the "house divided against itself cannot stand" campaign. The body/organism frame reaches its first rhetorical peak. Note the Alton debate (1858) cancer/body instance (inst_00132) — disease language applied to slavery-as-institution, explicitly refusing the purification-spread logic.

**Cluster_03 dormant**: Zero instances 1854–1860 despite maximum rhetorical activity. The experiment/proof frame plays no role in the anti-extension argument. Lincoln makes the inheritance argument in debates, not the evidentiary argument.

**Cluster_02, 04, 06 absent**: All war-phase clusters have zero instances in Phase 1.

---

### Phase 2: Obligation (1861)

The war's launch. Four documents, 25 instances. The most structurally important single year in terms of cluster shifts.

**Cluster_02 launches**: 7 instances in one year — the covenant/oath frame comes into existence the moment Lincoln takes the presidential oath. It was not available as a rhetorical resource before 1861 because Lincoln had no oath to defend. The obligatory_frame_rate of 1.000 makes the war feel mathematically necessary: the oath was sworn; it must be fulfilled.

**Cluster_03 reactivates**: 6 instances, all in the Annual Message to Congress (doc_010). The experiment/proof frame re-emerges for a legislative audience after 9 years of dormancy. The war is the decisive test.

**Cluster_06 begins**: 6 instances spread across Springfield Farewell (doc_008) and First Inaugural (doc_009). The Providence/theodicy frame is beginning its monotonic growth that will carry through 1865.

**Cluster_05 persists**: 4 instances in 1861 — the founding-fathers frame survives into the early war, still anchoring constitutional legitimacy. But its peak is behind it.

---

### Phase 3: Transformation (1862–1863)

Emancipation and Gettysburg. Six documents, 27 instances. The hinge phase.

**Cluster_04 arrives**: 3 instances at Gettysburg (1863-11-19). "Brought forth on this continent a new nation… new birth of freedom." The birth/creation frame makes its war-period debut at the moment of maximum symbolic import. Its 21-year dormancy ends.

**Cluster_05 terminates**: The single Gettysburg instance ("our fathers brought forth") is the last attestation in the corpus. The founding-fathers frame passes the torch at Gettysburg and does not return. After November 1863, Lincoln never invokes the founding generation again.

**Cluster_01 mid-range**: 5 instances in 1863. The body frame is mid-range — not at its 1858 or 1865 peaks.

**Greeley Letter suppression (1862)**: Cluster_02 deploys against emancipation — "save the Union" as covenant obligation. Documents the political flexibility and risk of the covenant frame.

---

### Phase 4: Theodicy (1864–1865)

The war's meaning. Five documents, 30 instances. The theodicy phase.

**Cluster_06 dominant**: 4 instances (1864) + 5 instances (1865) = 9 instances across the final 18 months. The Hodges Letter (1864) is the proto-Second-Inaugural: "I claim not to have controlled events." The Second Inaugural (1865) contains 4 cluster_06 instances — its single-document peak.

**Cluster_01 peaks in 1865**: 8 instances, all from the Second Inaugural. The body cluster transforms into its Reconstruction form — physician-president binding wounds. The 1865 body cluster is different from the 1858 body cluster: no longer house-divided warning, now programmatic healing.

**Cluster_03 terminates**: Last attestation November 1864 (doc_020). The experiment/proof frame does not appear in the Second Inaugural — the war's meaning is no longer evidentiary. Theodicy has supplanted proof.

**Cluster_05 absent**: Zero instances in 1864 and 1865. The founding generation does not appear in the Second Inaugural — the most important speech of Lincoln's presidency — at all.

## Guilt Distribution Arc (Primary Analytical Series)

The guilt_distribution arc is the most important single diachronic series: it tracks the structural transformation that makes "malice toward none" possible.

| Document | Date | Dominant guilt_distribution | Notes |
|----------|------|----------------------------|-------|
| doc_001 Lyceum | 1838 | external | The republic's enemies are abstract mob forces |
| doc_004 Peoria | 1854 | external | The slave power as external agent violating founders' intent |
| doc_005 House Divided | 1858 | external | Douglas conspiracy, slave-power extension |
| doc_007 Cooper Union | 1860 | external | Named founders testify against slavery extension |
| doc_009 First Inaugural | 1861 | external | Secessionists as covenant-breakers |
| doc_012 Greeley Letter | 1862 | external | Coalition-maintenance; guilt distributed to secession |
| doc_017 Gettysburg | 1863 | shifting | The dead prove the proposition; guilt not foregrounded |
| doc_019 Hodges Letter | 1864 | distributed (proto) | "I claim not to have controlled events" — agency distributed |
| doc_021 Second Inaugural | 1865 | distributed + cosmic | "Both read the same Bible" — guilt distributed across both sides; ultimately assigned to God's inscrutable purposes |

**Arc**: external (1838–1862) → shifting (1863) → distributed + cosmic (1864–1865)

**Register-controlled check**: Within formal_public_address alone (excluding debates and letters), the arc holds. First Inaugural (1861) is still external-guilt; Second Inaugural (1865) is distributed-cosmic. The arc is not an artifact of register shifts.

**Why this matters for Reconstruction**: "Malice toward none, charity for all" is not a moral aspiration tacked onto a punitive war — it is a structural consequence of the guilt_distribution arc. If both sides are guilty of the national sin of slavery, neither deserves malice. The distributed-guilt theodicy of the Second Inaugural structurally precludes a punitive Reconstruction.

## Cluster Co-activation at Key Moments

| Document | Date | Co-activated clusters | Significance |
|----------|------|-----------------------|--------------|
| doc_009 First Inaugural | 1861 | C01 + C02 + C06 | War-launch: body, covenant, and Providence simultaneously active |

**First Inaugural → Second Inaugural comparison**: Both documents activate clusters 01, 02, and 06. But the content is transformed:
- Cluster_01: body division (1861) → body healing (1865)
- Cluster_02: oath obligation to defend (1861) → oath registered in heaven as both parties' bond (1865)
- Cluster_06: God as witness (1861) → "Almighty has His own purposes" (1865)

The Second Inaugural is not a new metaphor system — it is the First Inaugural's metaphor system deepened by four years of war.

## Summary Answers to Narrative Questions

**1. Which cluster shows the strongest diachronic development?**
Cluster_06 (Providence/theodicy). First attested 1852 in a eulogistic form (providential provision), it grows through every phase of the war, achieving its most developed form at the Second Inaugural. Its trajectory is the most sustained and directional of any cluster.

**2. Which cluster is most stable?**
Cluster_01 (body/organism). It appears across the entire corpus (1838–1865), in every register, and transforms rather than disappears: from "suicide of the republic" (1838) through "house divided" (1858) through "binding up the nation's wounds" (1865). The body cluster is the backbone of the metaphor system.

**3. What is the relationship between guilt_distribution and the cluster_02 → cluster_06 shift?**
Cluster_02 (covenant) maintains external guilt throughout its lifespan — the covenant-breakers are the guilty party. Cluster_06 (Providence) enables distributed guilt — if both sides are instruments of God's punishment, guilt is shared. The shift from covenant (external guilt, legal frame) to theodicy (distributed guilt, theological frame) is what makes "malice toward none" structurally possible.

**4. Does cluster_04 (birth) displace or complement cluster_01 (wound) in Phase 4–5?**
Complement. Both are active at the Second Inaugural and Last Address. The wound is being healed; simultaneously, something new is being born. The cluster_01 peak in 1865 and the cluster_04 presence in 1865 are concurrent. They are not competing — the birth is what emerges from the healing.

**5. Where does disease_and_purification NOT appear?**
Everywhere. It is systematically absent from all 11 years of the corpus, all registers, all six clusters. The most notable absence points:
- 1858 debates: cancer language used for slavery (cluster_01) but explicitly refuses the purifying-spread logic
- 1863 Gettysburg: maximum sacrificial economy, maximum opportunity for purification logic — none
- 1865 Second Inaugural: maximum violence-authorization moment — no purification, only distributed guilt and restorative healing
