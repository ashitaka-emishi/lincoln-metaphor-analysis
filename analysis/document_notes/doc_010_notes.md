## Primary Finding: Phase 2 Baseline — Cluster_03 Reactivates as Dominant Frame

The July 4 Message (1861) is the Phase 2 baseline — the first congressional message after Fort Sumter, delivered on the first Independence Day of the war. It is the document where cluster_03_experiment_proposition reactivates as the dominant frame for the first time since the Lyceum Address (1838), and where that frame is applied directly to the crisis of war.

Active clusters: 02, 03, 05, 06 (four clusters — the most complex metaphor system in the corpus since the Lyceum Address). Absent: 01 (body/organism) and 04 (birth/creation).

The cluster profile reveals the register: a congressional message is a legal-constitutional argument, not an organic-body address. Lincoln argues logically (propositions, demonstrations, experiments) rather than biologically (wounds, healing, birth). The absence of cluster_01 in the July 4 Message — despite the war having begun three months earlier — is a register finding.

## Extension Group ext_016: The Experiment Arc (p51)

The most important single passage in doc_010 is the four-sentence experiment arc in p51:

1. **inst_00090** (p51_s01): Assertion — "Our popular government has often been called an experiment." The cluster_03 frame is explicitly named.
2. **inst_00091** (p51_s02): Partial proof — "Two points in it, our people have already settled---the successful establishing, and the successful administering of it." Two of three experimental criteria have been met.
3. **inst_00092** (p51_s03): Current test — "One still remains---its successful maintenance against a formidable [internal] attempt to overthrow it." The Civil War is the third experimental criterion.
4. **inst_00093** (p51_s04): Demonstration — "It is now for them to demonstrate to the world, that those who can fairly carry an election, can also suppress a rebellion." The proof is for a universal audience.

The arc converts the Civil War from a catastrophe into a logical proof. The three-point structure (two settled, one remaining) is a rhetorical enthymeme: partial proof creates the obligation to complete the demonstration.

## The Cluster_03 Diachronic Finding: 1838 → 1861 → 1863

The July 4 Message reveals a critical diachronic pattern:
- **1838 (Lyceum Address, inst_00082–inst_00084)**: "at first an undecided experiment; now a successful one." The experiment had already succeeded; Lincoln declares it complete.
- **1861 (July 4 Message, inst_00090–inst_00093)**: The experiment is reopened — the war has created a third criterion that remains unsettled.
- **1863 (Gettysburg)**: "testing whether that nation...can long endure" — the experiment frame reaches its highest rhetorical density.

Between 1838 and 1861 cluster_03 is dormant (absent from House Divided, Cooper Union, First Inaugural). The war reopens the experiment that the Lyceum Address had declared settled. This is the most significant diachronic cluster_03 finding in the corpus.

## inst_00087 (p16_s02): The Universal Audience Frame

Before the p51 arc, Lincoln establishes the experiment's stakes at p16_s02: the war "presents to the whole family of man, the question, whether a constitutional republic...can, or cannot, maintain its territorial integrity, against its own domestic foes."

The "whole family of man" as audience is a structural feature of the cluster_03 frame: the proof is not for Americans alone but for mankind. This anticipates Gettysburg's "that nation...dedicated to the proposition that all men are created equal" — the universal scope of the democratic experiment is established at Phase 2 and maintained through Phase 3.

## inst_00093: The Ballots/Bullets Parallel

The climactic statement of ext_016 contains a logical structure that will define Lincoln's war rhetoric: "those who can fairly carry an election, can also suppress a rebellion---that ballots are the rightful, and peaceful, successors of bullets." The ballots/bullets parallel asserts that electoral capacity and military capacity are the same democratic faculty — the competence to govern includes the competence to defend self-governance. This converts military action from aggression into democratic self-maintenance.

## The Sugar-Coating Passage (p35_s01): Disease Frame Not Deployed

p35_s01 — "With rebellion thus sugar-coated, they have been drugging the public mind of their section for more than thirty years" — is the clearest contamination/drugging language in doc_010. Lincoln characterizes the secessionists' strategy as a 30-year propaganda campaign that has "drugged" Southern public opinion into accepting rebellion.

This is not annotated as disease_and_purification in Lincoln's own metaphor system. The structure is identical to the Cooper Union p77_s05 case: Lincoln perceives and deploys the contamination frame as characterization of adversary strategy, not as his own governing logic. He does not prescribe purging, disinfection, or elimination of the contaminated element — he describes what was done to Southern public opinion by Southern leaders. The disease_and_purification frame remains absent from Lincoln's own system.

## Cluster_02: Plighted Faith (inst_00086)

p36_s07 — "The express plighting of faith, by each and all of the original thirteen, in the Articles of Confederation, two years later, that the Union shall [be perpetual]" — echoes inst_00072 in doc_009 (First Inaugural). Both documents cite the Articles of Confederation covenant as proof that the Union's perpetuity was plighted faith, not merely a political arrangement. In doc_010, this covenant argument serves Lincoln's legal-constitutional case against secession: the "plighting of faith" at the Articles of Confederation makes secession not merely illegal but covenant-breaking.

Note: p36_s07 is a split sentence (the segmenter broke it at "that the Union shall" with continuation in p37_s01: "be perpetual, is most conclusive"). Instance annotated on p36_s07 where "plighting of faith" appears.

## Clusters_05 Negative-Comparison Mode (inst_00088–inst_00089)

The two cluster_05 instances in p46 deploy the founding-fathers inheritance in a novel mode not seen in previous documents: the Confederate political acts are measured against the founding fathers' documents (Jefferson's Declaration, Washington's Constitution) and found inferior. Lincoln invokes the inherited patrimony not to claim it for himself but to show that the Confederacy has repudiated it.

- p46_s04: Confederate Declaration omits "all men are created equal" (Jefferson's patrimony).
- p46_s05: Confederate constitution omits "We, the People" (Washington's patrimony of popular sovereignty).

This is cluster_05 as negative proof: the inherited standard exposes the Confederacy's departure from the founding inheritance.

## Absent Clusters: Phase 2 Register Analysis

- **cluster_01 (body/organism) absent**: The nation is not addressed as a wounded or living body despite the war's onset. The congressional message register produces constitutional-logical argument. The organic frame belongs to public addresses, proclamations, and battlefield speeches — not congressional messages.
- **cluster_04 (birth/creation) absent**: No nativity language, no generative labor. The birth/new-creation frame is Gettysburg's contribution (Phase 3); the war has not yet been reframed as labor toward a new birth.

## Absence Flags

- `disease_and_purification`: **ABSENT** — confirmed; p35_s01 sugar-coating/drugging passage is characterization of Confederate propaganda strategy, not Lincoln's own ideological logic.
