## Primary Finding: Zero-Instance Forensic Document

The Charleston debate (September 18, 1858) is the most procedurally forensic of the seven debates. Lincoln's 632 Lincoln-authored sentences are dominated by:

1. **The Toombs bill conspiracy charge** (s01 and s03): Lincoln's primary task at Charleston is to defend Lyman Trumbull's charge that Douglas conspired to remove the submission-to-voters clause from the Toombs bill for Kansas statehood. Both of Lincoln's speeches are extended evidentiary arguments — citing specific documents, quoting bill language, disputing dates and amendments. This is documentary-forensic argumentation, not metaphor-rich rhetorical address.

2. **Racial hierarchy statements** (s01_p05–p08): Lincoln makes his most explicit statements on racial hierarchy at Charleston ("I am in favor of having the superior position assigned to the white race"), designed to neutralize Douglas's race-mixing charges at this politically hostile site (southern Illinois). These are defensive political assertions, not elevated rhetoric.

3. **The "wound up by a serenade"** (p114_s03): Superficially cluster_01, but "wound up" means "concluded," not a body metaphor.
