## Primary Finding: Systematic Suppression

The Greeley Letter is the most metaphorically sparse critical-priority document in the corpus. Lincoln uses "save the Union" eight or more times in a single paragraph and strips every entailment that normally accompanies the cluster_01 and cluster_02 framings.

**What is absent that is present everywhere else:**
- cluster_01: no body, no wound, no organism, no healing
- cluster_03: no proposition under test, no experiment
- cluster_04: no birth, no creation, no labor
- cluster_05: no founding fathers, no ancestral debt
- cluster_06: no Providence, no divine will, no theodicy

The letter contains almost no figurative language of any kind. It is written in the register of a legal contract negotiation: if-then clauses, stated premises, explicit conditions. The only elaborated metaphor is inst_00028 ("national authority can be restored"), which carries the restorative logic — the Union has a prior intact state worth recovering — but immediately drops it.

## The "Save" Repetition Pattern

"Save" appears in the following constructions across p05:
- "I would save the Union" (s01)
- "save it the shortest way" (s02)
- "national authority can be restored" / "the Union as it was" (s03)
- "save the Union" / "save slavery" (s03, twice)
- "save the Union" / "destroy slavery" (s04)
- "save the Union" (s05)
- "save it by freeing all the slaves" / "save it by freeing some" (s06, twice)
- "helps to save the Union" / "help to save the Union" (s07, twice)

The effect of this repetition is not rhetorical amplification but semantic evacuation. By applying the same verb ("save") to the Union, to slavery preservation, and to slavery destruction in exact grammatical parallel, Lincoln strips "save the Union" of its ideological content and reduces it to a utility calculation. This is the rhetorical function: public Union-preservation as mere pragmatic duty, not sacred covenant or organic necessity.

## Why the Suppression Matters

The letter was published on August 22, 1862. Lincoln had drafted the Preliminary Emancipation Proclamation one month earlier (July 22) and was waiting for a military victory to release it. He needed the public record to show that emancipation, when it came, was a military measure — not an ideological commitment. Any deployment of the covenant, birth, or Providence clusters would have tied emancipation to moral obligation and created political liability.

The suppression is therefore not absence of thought but a deliberate rhetorical strategy: clear the ideological frame before making the ideological move.

## Koenigsberg Constructs

**Fantasy type**: oath_and_obligation — the minimal residual structure. Lincoln's duty to save the Union is framed as a constitutional obligation, not a moral or theological one.

**Violence logic**: restorative (inst_00028) — the goal is return to prior state, not creation of something new. This directly contradicts the cluster_04 (birth/creation) logic Lincoln uses in Gettysburg (1863). The Greeley Letter is in the restorative phase; Gettysburg moves to the generative phase.

**Guilt distribution**: external — Confederacy is implied cause; not named or elaborated.

**Absence flags**: enslaved_people_non_agent throughout. The entire p05 paragraph discusses what to do "about slavery and the colored race" without ever treating enslaved people as agents with interests. They are the object of the grammatical clause ("save slavery" / "destroy slavery") — passive, interchangeable, the variable in Lincoln's utility equation.

## Diachronic Position

Document is Phase 3 (1862, mid-war) in the five-phase arc. Situated between:
- The First Inaugural (doc_009, 1861) — rich covenant framing, heavy cluster_02
- The Gettysburg Address (doc_017, 1863) — generative birth framing, cluster_04 dominant

The Greeley Letter is the suppression valley between these two peaks. Its metaphorical flatness is a function of political timing, not of Lincoln's rhetorical capability.

## Annotator Notes

- Only 2 instances for a critical-priority document. The floor number is itself the finding.
- inst_00028 ("the Union as it was") is analytically significant as the conservative restoration target — this is the restorative frame in its purest form. The Gettysburg Address will replace this target with "a new birth of freedom" (cluster_04). The shift from "the Union as it was" to "new birth" is one of the corpus's major diachronic moves.
- "the colored race" (p05_s07) — this is the most depersonalized reference to Black Americans in any Lincoln document analyzed so far. Not enslaved people, not freedmen, not citizens: "the colored race" as a categorical variable in Lincoln's utility function.
