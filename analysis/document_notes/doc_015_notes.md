## Primary Finding: More Stripped Than the Preliminary

The Final Emancipation Proclamation is more metaphorically stripped than the Preliminary (doc_013). The Preliminary had two instances of the cluster_02 formula "restoring the constitutional relation" (both at 0.62). The Final drops even that residue. The only surviving metaphor is the closing invocation — "the gracious favor of Almighty God" — which is cluster_06 at 0.78.

The document that makes emancipation operative — the most consequential legal act of Lincoln's presidency — is the most metaphorically minimalist document in the corpus. This is not rhetorical poverty. It is the legal register performing its maximum suppression function: enacting rather than arguing.

**What is absent:**
- cluster_01 (body/organism): no wound, no severance, no health language
- cluster_02 (covenant/oath): "restoring the constitutional relation" dropped entirely from doc_013
- cluster_03 (experiment/proposition): no proposition, no test, no demonstration
- cluster_04 (birth/creation): no nativity language
- cluster_05 (fathers/inheritance): no founding lineage

**What survives:** cluster_06, barely — one invocation of divine favor in the closing sentence.

## The God Invocation (inst_00054)

"I invoke the considerate judgment of mankind, and the gracious favor of Almighty God."

The sentence submits the act to two forms of validation: historical (mankind's judgment) and divine (God's favor). Lincoln is not claiming divine command — he requests God's "gracious favor," not God's mandate. This is the most theologically modest cluster_06 frame in the corpus: Providence as potential endorser rather than as commander or judge.

**Contrast with cluster_06 arc across the corpus:**
- Springfield Farewell (doc_008, Phase 1): Providence as companion-attendant ("that Being...will...send us...all")
- Hodges Letter (doc_019, Phase 3): Providence as director of history ("God wills the removal of a great wrong")
- Final Emancipation (doc_015, Phase 2): Providence as patron whose favor is sought ("gracious favor")
- Second Inaugural (doc_021, Phase 5): Providence as cosmic judge and punisher (blood-lash-sword economy)

The Final Emancipation sits between Phase 1 and Phase 3 in theological register, asking rather than asserting. The shift from "I invoke God's favor" to "God wills the removal of a great wrong" happens over the following 15 months.

## The Black Soldiers Clause (p12)

"Such persons of suitable condition, will be received into the armed service of the United States to garrison forts, positions, stations, and other places, and to man vessels of all sorts in said service."

This is the first legal authorization of Black military service in the Lincoln corpus. The framing is entirely administrative: "suitable condition" (medical/logistical intake criterion), "received into" (passive intake, not voluntary enlistment), task assignment limited to garrison and naval labor. The language of "garrison forts" and "man vessels" assigns Black soldiers to infrastructure functions, not combat roles.

`black_soldiers_absent` applies: Black soldiers are present as functional military assets, absent as agents with moral standing. The document that authorizes their service frames it as administrative intake.

## The Instruction to the Freed (p11)

"I hereby enjoin upon the people so declared to be free to abstain from all violence, unless in necessary self-defence; and I recommend to them that, in all cases when allowed, they labor faithfully for reasonable wages."

This is the only sentence in the corpus where Lincoln directly addresses Black people. The moment of emancipation is narrated as behavioral instruction: do not be violent; work for wages. `enslaved_people_non_agent` applies in its strongest form: freed people appear as recipients of commands, not as agents of their own liberation.

**The apple of gold again absent**: The Constitution Fragment (doc_011) theorized that the constitutional frame exists to preserve Liberty. The Final Emancipation enacts Liberty (for the enslaved in rebel states) and never mentions Liberty as a concept. The apple is missing from the most consequential act of its redemption.

## Diachronic Position

Document is Phase 2 (early wartime, January 1863) — three and a half months after the Preliminary (doc_013), six months before the Gettysburg Address (doc_017).

The cluster trajectory at this point:
- The Preliminary (September 1862) had the restoration formula (cluster_02, 0.62×2)
- The Final (January 1863) drops the restoration formula entirely; adds God invocation (cluster_06, 0.78)
- The Gettysburg Address (November 1863) will deploy all six clusters in concentrated form across 10 sentences

The gap between the Final Emancipation and Gettysburg represents the most extreme metaphor suppression → metaphor activation contrast in the corpus: one cluster at 0.78 becomes six clusters across 13 instances within 10 months.

## Quoted Passages (p05, p06)

Paragraphs p05 and p06 are Lincoln quoting the Preliminary Proclamation verbatim — editorial apparatus confirms they are pasted clippings with quotation marks added by Lincoln. The cluster_02 formula "restoring the constitutional relation" appears in p06 (quoted). Not re-annotated here — see doc_013 inst_00052 for the annotation in its original context.

The decision to paste rather than paraphrase is itself analytically interesting: Lincoln found no reason to restate the war aim in new language. The Preliminary's formula stood without elaboration.
