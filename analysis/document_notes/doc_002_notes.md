## Primary Finding: Earliest Cluster_04 in Corpus (1842)

The Temperance Address (February 22, 1842) contains the first cluster_04_birth_creation instances in the corpus. The Lyceum Address (1838, doc_001) had cluster_01, cluster_03, and cluster_05 — but no cluster_04. Here, at age 32, Lincoln first deploys the generative/birth frame for political liberty. The frame appears in two variants:

1. **Cultivation variant** (inst_00099, p28_s05; inst_00101, p31_s02): liberty as a germ that has germinated and grows; freedom as something planted and nurtured to maturity.
2. **Natal-origin variant** (inst_00100, p31_s01): America as the birthplace and cradle where the revolutions were born and initially nurtured.

The cultivation variant of 1842 is distinct from the obstetric variant that will appear at Gettysburg ("new birth of freedom," 1863). The diachronic arc of cluster_04 in Lincoln's metaphor system:
- 1842 (Temperance Address): germ/planted/cradle — cultivation frame
- 1863 (Gettysburg): "new birth of freedom" — obstetric frame
- 1865 (Second Inaugural): not present (cluster_06 dominates)

## Extension Group ext_017: The Cradle-and-Cultivation Arc (p31)

Two sentences within p31 form the cluster_04 arc:

1. **inst_00100** (p31_s01): Natal-origin — America as "the birth-place and the cradle of both those revolutions" (political and temperance). The revolutions are newborns born and cradled in American soil.
2. **inst_00101** (p31_s02): Cultivation outcome — the People who "planted, and nurtured to maturity, both the political and moral freedom of their species." The planting/nurturing converts the natal frame into a cultivation obligation: having given birth, America must tend the plants to maturity.

Together: birthplace (natal origin) → planted and nurtured to maturity (cultivation obligation). The two-sentence arc converts the completed act of birth into an ongoing obligation of cultivation.

## inst_00098 (p28_s04) + p29_s02: Earliest Cluster_03/Sacrificial-Cost Pairing

The cluster_03 instance at p28_s04 is immediately followed by p29_s02: "It breathed forth famine, swam in blood and rode in fire; and long, long after, the orphan's cry, and the widow's wail, continued to break the sad silence that ensued." Lincoln describes the Revolution's horrific costs immediately after praising its achievement. This is the earliest proof-plus-cost pairing in the corpus: the solution to the long-mooted problem was purchased at a price of blood and famine. The sacrificial economy is latently present: the proof cost suffering. This pairing anticipates Gettysburg's explicit logic — the experiment is proven by the dead who gave "the last full measure of devotion."

## The Disease/Purification Analysis: Critical Structural Finding

The Temperance Address contains the most explicit demon/contamination/death rhetoric in Lincoln's early corpus. Three notable passages:

- p27_s04: "The demon of intemperance ever seems to have delighted in sucking the blood of genius and of generosity."
- p27_s09: "he keeps our fathers, our brothers, our sons, and our friends, prostrate in the chains of moral death"
- p22_s03: "Drunken devils are cast out by ones, by sevens, and by legions"

This language is not annotated as `disease_and_purification` because the target of the contamination/demonic frame is ALCOHOL — an inanimate substance — not a group of people. Drinkers are consistently treated as VICTIMS of the demon, not as the demon itself. Lincoln repeatedly insists that drinkers are kind, generous, and charitable persons who have been afflicted ("pitied and compassionated"). The remedy is not elimination of the contaminated persons but their rescue from the contaminating substance.

This is the structural contrast with Hitlerian ideological fantasy: Lincoln's contamination language treats the afflicted as objects of compassion and rescue; the substance is the contamination. Hitler's contamination language treats the afflicted group as the contamination to be eliminated. Lincoln can deploy demonic/blood-sucking language freely when targeting an inanimate substance — he never deploys it against a human group.

`disease_and_purification` remains absent from Lincoln's metaphor system against people.

## The "Neither a Slave nor a Drunkard" Parallel (p31_s01)

p31_s01's "when there shall be neither a slave nor a drunkard on the earth" pairs the abolition of slavery with the abolition of drunkenness as dual goals of dual revolutions. This 1842 pairing is significant: Lincoln treats slavery and alcoholism as parallel bondages — both are moral evils from which humanity requires liberation. The framing does not yet make anti-slavery the primary political cause (that is 1854–1865); it treats them as equivalent moral urgencies. The "neither a slave nor a drunkard" formulation is the closest Lincoln comes in the early corpus to explicitly naming the liberation of enslaved people as a goal — but in the context of the temperance movement, not political action.

## Cluster_06 Near-Miss (p15_s04)

p15_s04 mentions "an over-ruling Providence" but only in the context of a philosophical argument: "The success of the argument in favor of the existence of an over-ruling Providence, mainly depends upon that sense [universal sense of mankind]." Lincoln is making a meta-argumentative point about how Providence-belief depends on universal consensus. He is not deploying Providence as a frame for political action. Confidence 0.60 — not annotated.

## Absent Clusters

- **cluster_01 (body/organism) absent**: The nation is not addressed as a living body. The organic language in the document (blood-sucking demon, moral death) targets alcohol/intemperance as the afflicting agent, not the nation as organism.
- **cluster_02 (covenant/oath) absent**: No sacred-bond or sworn-oath language. The temperance commitment is a personal pledge, not a constitutional covenant.
- **cluster_05 (fathers/inheritance) absent**: Washington is invoked at p32_s03 ("Washington is the mightiest name of earth") but without the patrimonial/inheritance mapping. He is honored, not cited as juridical precedent or authoritative bequest. The Speech is delivered on Washington's birthday but does not deploy the fathers-inheritance frame.
- **cluster_06 (Providence/theodicy) absent**: Providence appears only in philosophical meta-argument (p15_s04, conf 0.60), not as divine sanction for Lincoln's position.
