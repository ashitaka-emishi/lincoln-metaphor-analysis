## Primary Finding: Phase 0 Baseline — Earliest Cluster_03 in Corpus

The Lyceum Address (January 1838) is the Phase 0 baseline — Lincoln at age 28, twenty-five years before Gettysburg. Three clusters are active (01, 03, 05); clusters 02, 04, and 06 are absent. The document's most significant finding is that cluster_03_experiment_proposition appears here at its full logical strength, 25 years before Gettysburg's "testing whether that nation...can long endure." The experiment frame is not invented at Gettysburg — it is retrieved and redeployed. The 1838 Lyceum Address is its origin point in the corpus.

The developmental arc from doc_001 to Gettysburg:
- Lyceum (1838, Phase 0): 3 clusters (01, 03, 05), 8 instances
- House Divided (1858, Phase 1): 1 cluster (01), 3 instances — cluster_03 dormant 20 years
- Cooper Union (1860, Phase 1b): 1 cluster (05), 4 instances
- First Inaugural (1861, Phase 1c): 3 clusters (01, 02, 06), 6 instances
- Gettysburg (1863, Phase 3): 6 clusters, 13 instances — cluster_03 fully reactivated

The 20-year dormancy of cluster_03 between the Lyceum Address and Gettysburg is a significant finding: the experiment frame was available to Lincoln from the beginning but he did not deploy it during the political crisis years (1858–1861). It reactivates when the war's meaning requires a logical frame for what the dead have proven.

## Extension Group ext_014: The Experiment Arc (p17)

Three sentences within p17 form the corpus's earliest and most logically complete cluster_03 arc:

1. **inst_00082** (p17_s06): Temporal transition — "at first, it was but an experiment; now, it is understood to be a successful one." The proposition has moved from uncertain hypothesis to demonstrated conclusion.
2. **inst_00083** (p17_s08): Propositional content — "a practical demonstration of the truth of a proposition...the capability of a people to govern themselves." The experiment's subject is defined: democratic self-government.
3. **inst_00084** (p17_s12): Capstone declaration — "The experiment is successful." The simplest formulation. The proof is complete.

Together: temporal claim (hypothesis → conclusion) → content claim (what was proven) → declarative assertion (it is proven). The arc at p17 is logically self-contained. Gettysburg's "testing whether that nation...can long endure" is the same logical structure applied to the crisis of war: the experiment is now being re-run under fire, and the dead are proving it can succeed.

## Extension Group ext_015: The Inheritance Arc (p02)

Two sentences within p02 establish the patrimony frame:

1. **inst_00078** (p02_s03): Legal title — "We are the legal inheritors of these fundamental blessings." The generation receives a legal right, not merely a gift. The blessings are an estate.
2. **inst_00079** (p02_s04): Transfer narrative — the legacy "bequeathed us by a once hardy, brave, and patriotic, but now lamented and departed race of ancestors." The founding generation is now dead — their estate passes legally to the present generation.

The bequest frame activates an obligation: legal heirs must maintain and transmit the estate. Squandering the inheritance (through mob violence, inst_00081) is not only imprudent but a breach of fiduciary duty to the patrimony.

## inst_00081: The Novel Blood-Covenant Variant

The most novel cluster_05 instance in doc_001 — and one of the most novel in the full corpus — is inst_00081 (p12_s03): "whenever the vicious portion of population shall be permitted to gather in bands of hundreds and thousands, and burn churches, ravage and rob provision-stores, throw printing presses into rivers, shoot editors, and hang and burn obnoxious persons at pleasure, and with impunity; depend on it, this Government cannot last. By such things, the feelings of the best citizens will become more or less alienated from it; and thus it will be left without friends, or with too few, and those few too weak, to make their support effectual. At every step we tread, we tread on the blood of his father." (span: "trample on the blood of his father" — the concluding formulation)

The blood-covenant variant maps: the ground of civic life is the blood sacrifice of the founding generation. To permit mob violence (trample the law) is literally to desecrate the martyrs' blood underfoot. This is cluster_05 in its most visceral form — the inheritance is not an abstract estate but a blood covenant written into the physical ground of the republic. No other document in the corpus uses "blood of his father" in this way.

## Cluster_01 in doc_001: Mortal-Organism Variant

inst_00080 (p04_s05) — "we must die by suicide" — introduces cluster_01 in its mortal-organism variant at the earliest corpus point. The nation has life; it can die; and the specific death-mode available to it is self-inflicted (suicide). External conquest is excluded. This is the earliest appearance of the nation-as-mortal-organism mapping in the corpus.

Key entailment: if the nation can only die by suicide, then internal dissolution (mob violence, disrespect for law) is the only genuine existential threat. External enemies are structurally secondary. This entailment will reappear — transformed — at Gettysburg: "that nation...dedicated to the proposition that all men are created equal" can be killed only by failing the self-governance proposition. The internal-threat structure persists from 1838 to 1863.

## Absent Clusters: 1838 Baseline

- **cluster_02 (covenant/oath) absent**: Lincoln has not yet developed the constitutional-as-sacred-covenant register. The Constitution is invoked as law, not as sworn bond. The covenant register belongs to the Inaugural addresses (1861).
- **cluster_04 (birth/creation) absent**: No nativity language, no generative act, no labor metaphor. The republic has already been born; Lincoln is 25; the war is 23 years away. No occasion yet for crisis-as-birth-labor.
- **cluster_06 (Providence/theodicy) absent**: No divine sanction, no God's judgment, no providential frame. The Lyceum Address is explicitly secular — Lincoln argues from reason and institutional design, not from divine will. Providence will not enter the corpus until the First Inaugural (1861).

## The Disease_and_Purification Near-Miss (p09_s05)

p09_s05 contains language that superficially resembles a purification frame: Lincoln describes how mob violence produces "dead men's bones" and "savage mobs" and the destruction of civic order. The passage characterizes the problem as moral corruption, but Lincoln's frame is institutional-mechanical (law as a system that fails when disrespected), not purificatory. He does not prescribe purging, cleansing, or elimination of a diseased element. The remedy he proposes is reverence for law as political religion. `disease_and_purification` is absent from Lincoln's own metaphor system; the near-miss is an institutional-decay frame, not a disease-and-cure frame.

## Diachronic Position

January 1838 — Phase 0 (pre-political, pre-corpus baseline). This document predates the political crisis years by 20 years. Its primary value is demonstrating which clusters were available to Lincoln from the outset of his rhetorical career:
- cluster_01 (mortal organism): available 1838
- cluster_03 (experiment/proposition): fully articulated 1838, dormant 1838–1863, reactivated at Gettysburg
- cluster_05 (fathers/inheritance): juridical and blood-covenant variants, both available 1838

The experiment frame's 25-year arc — from "The experiment is successful" (1838) to "testing whether that nation...can long endure" (1863) — is the corpus's most significant diachronic metaphor finding.
