## Three-Arc Extended Metaphor Structure

**ext_001 — Birth/death/rebirth arc** (inst_00001, 00002, 00004, 00008, 00011, 00012)
- Opens: nation conceived and brought forth (S1)
- Midpoint: soldiers gave their lives / nation as mortal body (S3, S6)
- Resolution: "new birth of freedom" (S8)
- Logic: sacrifice of the first-generation bodies enables the nation's second birth

**ext_002 — Oath/dedication arc** (inst_00007, 00009)
- Opens: living dedicated to "unfinished work" (S4, S7)
- Resolution: implicit — the dedication completes the oath broken by secession
- Koenigsberg function: converts free action into obligation; war feels unavoidable

**ext_003 — Proposition/test arc** (inst_00003, 00005, 00013)
- Opens: nation = proposition (S1)
- Midpoint: proposition under test by war (S2)
- Resolution: proposition proven by survival (S8)
- Logic: the war is the proof procedure; winning = logical Q.E.D.

**Key structural finding**: All three arcs resolve simultaneously in the final sentence (S8 = sentence 10 of 10). The compressed simultaneity creates the rhetorical effect of inevitability — birth, oath, and proof all require the same war.

## Koenigsberg Constructs

**Fantasy type**: sacrificial_economy — soldier deaths are not losses but inputs; the nation's survival is the output. No death exceeds its function.

**Violence logic**: purification_through_sacrifice — specifically organic rebirth, not disease expulsion. This is the critical distinction from Hitler's framework.

**Obligatory frame**: covenant_violation — the founding proposition is "tested" because secession attempted to falsify it; the war is the logical refutation.

**Guilt distribution**: external — Confederacy is implicit cause; Lincoln does not name actors.

**Projected entity**: none prominent — this is the earliest of the annotated documents and projects least.

**Sacrificial economy**: closed and complete. inst_00006 + inst_00012: the dead gave their lives so the nation might have a new birth. Exchange is named explicitly.
