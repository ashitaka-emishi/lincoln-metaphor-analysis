## Primary Finding: Most Cluster_05-Concentrated Major Document in Corpus

The Cooper Union Address activates only one cluster across its entire 7,700 words — cluster_05_fathers_inheritance — with clusters 01, 02, 03, 04, and 06 entirely absent. This is the corpus's most cluster-restricted major address after the House Divided Speech (doc_005), and the document that establishes the fathers-inheritance frame as the argumentative backbone of Lincoln's constitutional case in the pre-war period.

The speech's entire first section is a historical enumeration: of the thirty-nine signers of the Constitution, Lincoln identifies twenty-one who voted in contexts that authorized federal control of slavery in the territories; the remaining eighteen left no contrary evidence. The fathers are not inspirational figures here — they are legal precedents. Their votes are evidence, their authority is a ruling, their policy is binding. The inheritance obligation is juridical.

## The "Our Fathers" Formula: Juridical Variant of Cluster_05

The phrase "our fathers, who framed the Government under which we live" appears more than ten times in the speech. Four instances were annotated; the remainder are threshold mentions or evidentiary repetitions without adding new cluster_05 mapping. The four annotated instances represent the full range of the juridical variant:

1. **inst_00068** (p37_s03): The authority-as-override-threshold — the fathers' authority governs unless evidence is conclusive enough to overturn it. The burden of proof is on those who would depart from the inheritance.
2. **inst_00069** (p39_s02, ext_013): The behavioral inheritance imperative — if you concede the fathers understood the question better, you are obligated to speak as they spoke and act as they acted. Epistemic deference generates behavioral obligation.
3. **inst_00070** (p39_s04, ext_013): The policy-marking inheritance — as the fathers marked slavery's status (evil not to be extended, tolerated only by necessity), so must it be marked again. The inheritance is a specific policy designation, not just general authority.
4. **inst_00071** (p69_s01): The capstone judicial precedent — the fathers decided this same constitutional question, without division, without ambiguity about meaning, without factual error. The precedent is binding. It favors the Republicans.

## Extension Group ext_013: The Inheritance-Imperative Arc

inst_00069 and inst_00070 form a two-sentence arc within p39:
- **inst_00069** (p39_s02): behavioral obligation — speak as they spoke, act as they acted
- **inst_00070** (p39_s04): policy-specific obligation — as they marked it, so let it be again marked

Together they constitute the inheritance imperative's full statement: first the general behavioral obligation (replicate their actions), then the specific policy content (the tolerate-not-extend formula). The arc converts Douglas's formula about the fathers' superior understanding into a binding Republican commitment.

## The Rhetorical Judo of inst_00069

The most rhetorically sophisticated move in the Cooper Union address is inst_00069. Lincoln takes Douglas's own formula — "our fathers, who framed the Government under which we live, understood this question just as well, and even better, than we do now" — and converts it into a behavioral imperative that obligates the speaker to the Republican position. The logic is a trap: if you concede the fathers understood it better, you must act as they acted; and they authorized federal control of slavery in the territories. Any speaker who invokes founding authority against the Republican position has already committed to the Republican position, because that is what the founders practiced.

## The Disease_and_Purification Near-Appearance: Critical Koenigsberg Finding

p77_s05 contains the most explicit disease/purification language in the Cooper Union address: "The whole atmosphere must be disinfected from all taint of opposition to slavery." Lincoln is quoting — sarcastically — the Southern demand. He reports this as the extremist position Lincoln's opponents have reached: slavery must be so thoroughly entrenched that even opposition to it is treated as a contamination requiring disinfection.

This is **not** an annotatable instance for Lincoln's own metaphor system. Lincoln perceives and articulates the purification frame precisely — he uses it to characterize Southern extremism — but he never deploys it as his own governing logic. This is the key structural finding for the Koenigsberg comparison:

- Lincoln can describe the disease_and_purification frame from outside it
- He uses it as evidence of his opponents' radicalism, not as his own ideological logic
- `disease_and_purification` is absent from Lincoln's own metaphor system throughout the corpus
- The clearest near-appearance in the entire corpus (p77_s05) is a *reported* adversary frame, not Lincoln's frame

This distinction — between perceiving the purification frame and inhabiting it — is the critical structural difference between Lincoln's ideological operation and Hitler's, as analyzed by Koenigsberg.

## Why Only Cluster_05?

The cluster profile reflects the Phase 1b register and argument type:

- **cluster_01 (body/organism) absent**: The nation is not wounded or endangered as a living body. The constitutional question is legal, not biological. The body/organism frame will emerge when the war makes the nation's life the explicit stake.
- **cluster_02 (covenant/oath) absent**: The Constitution is treated as historical precedent, not a sacred bond. Lincoln argues from what the fathers *did*, not from what the Constitution *is* as a covenant. The covenant register belongs to the Inaugural addresses.
- **cluster_03 (experiment/proposition) absent**: Lincoln's argument is evidentiary — here is what the founders voted — not experimental. He is not asking whether democratic government can survive a test; he is presenting a historical record. The experiment frame belongs to Gettysburg's "testing whether..." formulation.
- **cluster_04 (birth/creation) absent**: No nativity language, no generative act. The republic is not being born here; it has already been founded, and the question is what the founders decided.
- **cluster_06 (Providence/theodicy) absent**: The speech is entirely secular-historical. No God's judgment, no divine sanction. Providence will not appear until the war begins and its meaning requires theological framing.

## Diachronic Position

February 1860 — Phase 1b (pre-war constitutional argument). Between the House Divided Speech (June 1858, Phase 1, cluster_01 only) and the First Inaugural (March 1861, Phase 1c, to be annotated). The developmental arc continues:
- House Divided (1858): 1 cluster (cluster_01), architectural frame
- Cooper Union (1860): 1 cluster (cluster_05), juridical frame
- First Inaugural (1861): to be annotated — expected to show cluster_02 activation as war approaches

The Cooper Union Address and House Divided Speech are both single-cluster documents, but the active cluster differs: architectural structure (cluster_01) vs. founding precedent (cluster_05). The two documents together establish the Phase 1 baseline before the war converts Lincoln's metaphor system into its full six-cluster form.
