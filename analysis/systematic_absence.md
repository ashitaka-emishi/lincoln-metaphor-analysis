## Primary Finding: `disease_purification_absent` (56 instances)

### The Central Structural Finding

`disease_purification_absent` is the largest absence category in the corpus with 56 instances — spanning **all six clusters**, **all phases** of the corpus (1838–1862 tracked in by_year data; all clusters through 1865), and **all registers**. No other flag achieves this scope.

This flag marks every instance where the metaphor's source domain made disease/purification logic available but Lincoln did not deploy it. The repeated non-deployment across 27 years and 136 instances constitutes a structural pattern, not coincidence.

**Definition**: An instance receives `disease_purification_absent` when: (a) the source domain involves body, organism, purity, covenant, founding lineage, divine judgment, or birth/creation, and (b) the entailments structurally permit applying disease/contamination/purification logic to a social group, and (c) Lincoln does not do so.

The non-deployment is **register-independent**. It is not that Lincoln avoids disease/purification logic in formal speeches while using it in debates or letters. The absence holds across every register.

### Mechanism: Why This Is Structurally Significant

Disease/purification logic is the fantasy type that makes genocide feel medically necessary. When a social group is constructed as a pathogen infecting the national body:

1. The national body cannot be healthy while the pathogen remains
2. The purification is never complete while any carrier survives
3. The violence has no exit condition — it must continue until the pathogen is fully expelled

This is the fantasy structure Richard Koenigsberg identifies in Hitler's speeches as the ideological engine of the Holocaust.

Lincoln's corpus has **zero instances** of this fantasy type. The 56 flags mark every opportunity where it was structurally available — and Lincoln took a different path at each one:

- **Alton debate (1858)**: Cancer language for slavery-as-institution, but explicitly: spreading would not be a cure. The anti-purification logic is stated directly.
- **Gettysburg (1863)**: Maximum sacrificial economy, maximum opportunity for purification framing (the war could purify the national body of its slave-holding element) — Lincoln frames it as proof and new birth instead.
- **Second Inaugural (1865)**: Maximum violence-authorization moment — Lincoln distributes guilt to both sides and assigns causation to God's inscrutable purposes. No purification, no pathogen, no enemy as disease.

### Analytical Significance

The `disease_purification_absent` finding is the project's central negative result. The argument:

1. Lincoln's corpus spans 27 years and 136 instances across 6 clusters
2. In every cluster, every phase, every register, when disease/purification logic was structurally available, Lincoln did not deploy it
3. This non-deployment is structural — built into the metaphor system's architecture
4. Every fantasy type in Lincoln's corpus has a logical terminus: the wound heals, the experiment concludes, the oath is fulfilled, the debt is paid, God's punishment runs its course
5. Disease/purification logic (absent from Lincoln, central to Hitler) has no terminus — it drives totalizing violence indefinitely
6. "Malice toward none" is not merely a moral aspiration; it is a structural consequence of a metaphor system that has no mechanism for constructing permanent enemies

## Secondary Finding: `enslaved_people_non_agent` (47 instances)

### The Structural Exclusion

`enslaved_people_non_agent` marks every instance where enslaved people (or freed people, or Black Americans) are structurally present in the metaphor's target domain but absent from the agent roles the entailments make available.

47 instances — 32.6% of all absence flags — across **all six clusters** and **all phases** from 1854 to 1865.

### Distribution by Cluster

| Cluster | Count | Role being denied | Key evidence |
|---------|-------|------------------|--------------|
| cluster_05 fathers | 16 | Inheritors of the founding legacy | Enslaved people cannot inherit what their exclusion produced |
| cluster_02 covenant | 10 | Covenant parties | Enslaved people were not Constitutional signatories |
| cluster_01 body | 10 | Healers; restored patients with agency | Absent from the healing scenario |
| cluster_06 providence | 5 | Agents of divine providence | Appear as subjects of punishment, not agents of redemption |
| cluster_03 experiment | 4 | Provers of the democratic proposition | The proposition is about them; they cannot prove it |
| cluster_04 birth | 2 | Agents of the new birth | The new birth names their freedom but not their agency |

### Distribution by Year

| Year | Count |
|------|-------|
| 1854 | 1 |
| 1858 | 13 |
| 1861 | 6 |
| 1862 | 4 |
| 1863 | 10 |
| 1864 | 6 |
| 1865 | 7 |

**Counterintuitive finding**: `enslaved_people_non_agent` actually **increases** after emancipation (1863: 10; 1864: 6; 1865: 7 — 23 of 47 instances post-1863). The Emancipation Proclamation does not resolve the structural exclusion; it intensifies the irony. As emancipation becomes real, the metaphor system's failure to accommodate Black agents becomes more visible.

### The Gettysburg Irony

The Gettysburg Address (November 1863) was delivered two months after the 54th Massachusetts's assault on Fort Wagner, with approximately 180,000 Black Union soldiers serving. It activates:

- cluster_03: "testing whether that nation can long endure" — Black soldiers are literally proving the proposition with their bodies
- cluster_04: "new birth of freedom" — Black soldiers' deaths are literally purchasing the new birth
- cluster_05: "our fathers brought forth" — Black Americans have as much stake in the founding legacy as white Americans

Black soldiers are the most literal embodiment of all three war-period clusters. They do not appear. `black_soldiers_erased` flag: 1 instance in doc_017.

### Analytical Significance

The `enslaved_people_non_agent` finding reveals the limits of Lincoln's civic framework — not a personal failing but a structural feature of the metaphor system. The categories the metaphors rely on (citizen, heir, prover, healer, covenant party) had de facto membership that excluded Black Americans even when the de jure logic of the Declaration required their inclusion.

The metaphors could not accommodate agents the political system had not yet recognized. The 13th Amendment, Reconstruction citizenship, and the 14th Amendment would expand that membership — but Lincoln did not live to develop the metaphor system that could accommodate it.

## `lincoln_non_agent` (19 instances)

19 instances; 63% concentrated in cluster_06 (Providence), where the divine-instrument entailment structurally effaces Lincoln's agency.

| Cluster | Count |
|---------|-------|
| cluster_06 | 12 |
| cluster_02 | 3 |
| cluster_01 | 3 |
| cluster_03 | 1 |

**By year**: 1861: 2, 1863: 6, 1864: 4, 1865: 7 — grows through the war, peaking in 1865 (the Second Inaugural).

**Mechanism**: The obligatory frame generates lincoln_non_agent as a structural byproduct. If Lincoln is fulfilling an oath (cluster_02), he has no discretion. If Lincoln is God's instrument (cluster_06), God decides. The agent-erasure is built into the fantasy types: `oath_and_obligation` and `punishment_and_theodicy` both position Lincoln as executor rather than author.

**Political function**: Lincoln-as-non-agent places the war's moral weight outside Lincoln's personal accountability. The 600,000 deaths are the oath's requirement, God's punishment for slavery, the experiment's cost — not Lincoln's decision. The self-effacement is psychically protective and politically useful simultaneously.

**Epistemic test**: The Blind Memorandum (doc_018, 1864, fragment_private) contains a cluster_06 instance in a private, no-audience context. The lincoln_non_agent pattern appearing in private confirms the sincerity of the public self-effacement.

## `death_abstracted` (8 instances)

8 instances; all formal_public_address; concentrated at Gettysburg and the Second Inaugural (1863: 7; 1865: 1).

Deaths enter the metaphor system as evidence (cluster_03), sacrifice yield (cluster_04), covenant cost (cluster_02), or divine punishment (cluster_06) without individual acknowledgment. The abstraction is structural to the sacrificial economy: named individuals cannot be converted into national investment without remainder.

---

## `confederates_depersonalized` (5 instances)

5 instances; cluster_01 (3), cluster_03 (1), cluster_06 (1).

Confederates appear in the metaphor system as abstract forces — the rebellion, the slave power, the covenant-breakers — not as individual persons. This is structurally consistent with the restorative violence logic: if the wound is reparable and the goal is healing, the wound-causers need not be named as permanent enemies. Depersonalization enables reconciliation; naming enemies enables permanent enmity.

## `women_absent` (1 instance)

One flag in cluster_04 (birth/creation). The birth metaphor borrows its logic from female reproductive labor while making women structurally invisible. "Bringing forth a new nation" — but the labor is attributed to founders, soldiers, and presidents, not to women. The single flag understates the structural irony; the conservative annotation threshold likely prevented additional flags in other birth instances.

## Synthesis: Three Layers of Structural Absence

The 144 flags reveal a metaphor system organized around **civic-national projection** that systematically excludes at three levels:

**1. Excluded agents**: Enslaved and freed Black Americans are present as objects of the metaphors (the proposition being tested, the wound being healed, the inheritance at stake, the people for whom freedom is born) but absent from agentive roles. 47 flags. This is a failure of the civic framework's de facto scope — a structural limitation the metaphor system could not, in Lincoln's lifetime, overcome.

**2. Displaced agency**: Lincoln himself is absent from agent roles in Providence and oath instances — positioned as instrument rather than decision-maker. 19 flags. This displacement is both psychically protective and politically legitimating: it converts political will into cosmic necessity.

**3. Refused fantasy type**: Disease/purification logic is structurally available throughout the corpus but never deployed against any social group. 56 flags. This is not an agent being excluded but a fantasy type being refused — and it is the refusal that makes reconciliation structurally possible. Where other political rhetorics found pathogens to expel, Lincoln found wounds to heal, propositions to prove, oaths to fulfill, debts to pay, and punishments to endure.

These three absences are not equivalent in their political valence:
- The exclusion of Black agents is a limitation — it forecloses the full emancipatory potential of Lincoln's own claims
- The displacement of Lincoln's agency is a resource — it enables the war's authorization without personal hubris
- The refusal of purification logic is the structural achievement — it is what separates Lincoln's rhetoric from the rhetoric that drives genocide
