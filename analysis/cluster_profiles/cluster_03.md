**Expected trajectory**: The experiment/proposition frame is Lincoln-specific — it does not appear in the early pre-political documents and reaches full development at Peoria (1854). It is present in administrative (congressional) register as well as formal addresses, which is analytically unusual. The July 4 Message (doc_010) is a key test: how does the logical-proof metaphor function in administrative prose?

The Re-election Serenade (doc_020) is particularly important: Lincoln spontaneously deploys the experiment frame to interpret the election result as empirical evidence. This is the unguarded, extemporaneous form of the cluster — highest epistemic value for genuine cognition.

## Political and Moral Work

The experiment/proposition frame does three pieces of work simultaneously:
1. **Universalizes the stakes**: the Civil War is not just an American problem but a test of democratic governance everywhere. This gives Lincoln's rhetoric a global audience and global significance.
2. **Converts defeat into disproof**: Confederate victory would not just be a military loss — it would *prove* that self-government is impossible. This makes surrender epistemologically impossible, not merely politically unacceptable.
3. **Makes war feel necessary rather than chosen**: if the experiment is underway, it must be completed. You cannot abandon an experiment midway.

---

## What the Metaphor Conceals

- **Who are the experimental subjects?**: The enslaved people whose status is what the proposition is *about* — they are not positioned as scientists or provers but as the matter under examination. The scientific metaphor requires an object of study; they fill that role.
- **The proposition's scope**: "all men are created equal" — Lincoln interprets this as applying to enslaved people in principle but defers it in practice (Greeley Letter). The experiment frame papers over this deferral.
- **The possibility that the proposition is already falsified**: if the Union spent four years maintaining slavery while claiming to prove equality, the experimental results are already compromised.

---

## Hitler Comparison

**No direct Hitler parallel**. Hitler does not use logical-proof or scientific-test metaphors for violence. His violence logic is purifying, not evidentiary. This is the most Lincoln-specific cluster.

**Structural note**: The experiment frame implicitly acknowledges that the proposition might be false — experiments can fail. This acknowledgment of fallibility is absent from Hitler's certainty about racial hierarchy. Lincoln's epistemic humility (the proposition might be disproved) is structurally related to his capacity for "malice toward none."
