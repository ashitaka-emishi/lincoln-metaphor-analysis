## Political and Moral Work

The theodicy frame does what no other cluster can do: it distributes guilt without distributing blame. "Both sides" are being punished for shared complicity in slavery — but this is God's judgment, not Lincoln's. This allows Lincoln to say "malice toward none" without it being merely rhetorical.

**The political necessity of cosmic guilt**: By Phase 5, Lincoln needs a framework that can accommodate Reconstruction without requiring permanent punishment of the South. The cosmic guilt distribution (God punishes both sides) provides this — it converts guilt into shared suffering already being paid, rather than blame requiring ongoing enforcement.

**The theodicy frame and lincoln_non_agent**: If Lincoln is an instrument of Providence, he is not responsible for what Providence requires. This gives him room to pursue hard policies (war, emancipation) without claiming them as his own choices.

---

## What the Metaphor Conceals

- **Human agency and political failure**: the slide toward war involved choices, political failures, moral compromises, and human decisions at every stage. The providence frame converts these contingent human acts into divine necessity.
- **The specific agents of slavery**: if slavery is a "national sin" for which everyone is being punished, the specific beneficiaries of slavery — the slaveholders — lose their particular moral responsibility in the cosmic distribution.
- **Theodicy's dangerousness**: the same theological framework that distributes guilt and opens toward reconciliation (Lincoln's version) can also justify unlimited violence ("God wills it" as a blank check). Track whether any instance tips into the dangerous form.

## Hitler Comparison

**Hitler parallel**: Providence / historical destiny
**Shared element**: Both invoke providential necessity; both position themselves as instruments of forces beyond their control; both use divine/historical destiny to generate obligatory framing.

**Key structural divergence**:
- Lincoln's Providence distributes guilt across both sides and opens toward reconciliation ("malice toward none")
- Hitler's Providence selects the Aryan people as chosen and marks others for elimination
- Lincoln's theodicy is a shared punishment; Hitler's is a unique destiny
- Lincoln's Providence is morally complex ("the Almighty has His own purposes" — God's ways are inscrutable); Hitler's Providence is completely legible (the Jews are the obstacle to Aryan destiny)

**The structural significance**: Lincoln's inscrutable God generates humility and limits violence ("let us judge not, that we be not judged"). Hitler's certain Providence generates boundless violence ("history commands it").
