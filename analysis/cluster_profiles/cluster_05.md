**Expected trajectory**: The fathers/inheritance cluster appears earliest of all — the Lyceum Address (1838) is already structured around the obligation to preserve what the founding generation built. ## Political and Moral Work

The fathers cluster does two things the other clusters cannot do as efficiently:
1. **Establishes historical continuity**: the war is not a rupture but a continuation of the founding project. Lincoln is not doing something new — he is completing what the founders began.
2. **Naturalizes the obligation**: you do not choose your parents or their debts. The obligation to the founders is as natural as filial obligation — it does not require rational justification.

**The idealization problem**: Lincoln's use of the founding fathers requires idealizing them. The Cooper Union address (doc_007) is a legal argument about what the founders intended regarding slavery. Lincoln argues they intended to restrict it — but this requires selective reading of the historical record. The idealization psychic defense is most visible here.

---

## What the Metaphor Conceals

- **The founders' ambivalence on slavery**: Jefferson owned enslaved people while writing "all men are created equal." The fathers cluster requires bracketing this contradiction.
- **Who counts as an heir**: the inheritance passes through the civic line (citizens), which in 1776 excluded enslaved people. The cluster naturalizes this exclusion.
- **The possibility that the fathers were wrong**: if the founders' intent on slavery was to restrict and eventually end it (Lincoln's argument), why did they build the three-fifths clause into the Constitution? The fathers cluster requires treating their intent as coherent and admirable.

## Hitler Comparison

**Hitler parallel**: Germanic ancestors / Aryan heritage
**Shared elements**: Both invoke an idealized founding/ancestral generation; both construct the present generation as obligated debtors to the past; both use `idealization` as the primary psychic defense.

**Key structural divergence**:
- Lincoln's founders are civic-rational — they wrote documents, made arguments, took oaths. Anyone can in principle join the civic lineage (immigrants, freed people).
- Hitler's ancestors are racial-biological — you are born into Aryan lineage or you are not. The inheritance cannot be acquired.
- Lincoln's idealization conceals the founders' compromises; Hitler's idealization constructs a racial purity that never existed historically.
